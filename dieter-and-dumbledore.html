Dieter & Dumbledore: A Foundation of Prophets and Apostles

My kids and I are currently in the middle of a Harry Potter reading marathon.

As I’m reading this series this time, I’m particularly impressed by the strength of character of Professor Dumbledore, the headmaster of Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. Everyone feels safe, knowing he is there. After all, he’s the only wizard that the evil Voldemort is afraid of.

Here's one description of Dumbledore:

“Professor Dumbledore, though very old, always gave an impression of great energy. He had several feet of long silver hair and beard, half-moon spectacles, and an extremely crooked nose. He was often described as the greatest wizard of the age, but that wasn't why Harry respected him. You couldn't help trusting Albus Dumbledore, and as Harry watched him beaming around at the students, he felt really calm for the first time.”1

From the author's mouth:

JK Rowling even said in an interview: “Dumbledore is the epitome of goodness.” 2

Quiz time:

One of my very most favorite little quizzes on the internet is Who said it: Dieter or Dumbledore?3

The quiz has quotes from two men: Dumbledore and Elder Dieter F. Uchtdorf, a modern apostle of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints.

After doing this quiz for the first time, I think I might have gotten a B.

Real-world equivalent:

Back to how Dumbledore was so strong and such a source of hope and security to those around him. Well, the apostles and prophets, and the priesthood power, could be considered our real-world equivalent.

The apostle Paul describes us as “no more strangers and foreigners, but fellow citizens with the saints, and of the household of God...built upon the foundation of the apostles and prophets, Jesus Christ himself being the chief cornerstone.”21

I love the image of a foundation. The apostles and prophets are called to be special witnesses of Jesus Christ, and they help us to anchor ourselves to Him, so “that when the devil shall send forth his mighty winds, yea, his shafts in the whirlwind, yea, when all his hail and his mighty storm shall beat upon you, it shall have no power over you to drag you down to the gulf of misery and endless wo, because of the rock upon which ye are built, which is a sure foundation, a foundation whereon if men build they cannot fall.”22

And we will be like the man “which built an house, and digged deep, and laid the foundation on a rock: and when the flood arose, the stream beat vehemently upon that house, and could not shake it: for it was founded upon a rock.”23

Special witnesses:

I am very grateful for these special witnesses of Jesus Christ. President Joseph F. Smith, one of the modern-day prophets, explained:

“These twelve disciples of Christ are supposed to be eye and ear witnesses of the divine mission of Jesus Christ. It is not permissible for them to say, I believe, simply; I have accepted it simply because I believe it. Read the revelation, the Lord informs us they must know, they must get the knowledge for themselves. It must be with them as though they had seen with their eyes and heard with their ears and they know the truth.”
“That is their mission, to testify of Jesus Christ and him crucified and risen from the dead and clothed now with almighty power at the right hand of God, the Savior of the world. That is their mission...to preach to the world, and see that it is preached to the world. Where they cannot go themselves, they are to have the help of others...under their direction to preach the gospel to the world and to declare the truth.”24
Joseph F. Smith at 19 years old Joseph F. Smith in 1905
